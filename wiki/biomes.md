## Detailed Biome Profiles

### Temperate Forest
- Temperature: -25C to 35C (summer ~25C, winter ~0C)
- Animals: deer, cougar, grizzly bear, lynx, timber wolf, bison, horse
- Flora: oak, poplar, berry bush, wild healroot
- Terrain: mostly soil + rich soil patches
- **Best all-around. Reliable growing, good foraging, moderate threats.**

### Arid Shrubland
- Temperature: 0C to 35C
- Animals: elephant, ostrich, dromedary, cougar, bison
- Low disease, decent growing. **Less wood** — plan stone/steel building early.

### Boreal Forest
- Temperature: -35C to 25C (summer ~10C, winter ~-10C)
- Growing season: 10-30/60 days
- Animals: arctic wolf, caribou, moose, elk, grizzly bear
- **Short growing season. Must stockpile food for winter. Good hunting.**

### Tropical Rainforest
- Temperature: 0C to 35C (never freezes)
- Year-round growing but **very high disease** (1.7/year)
- Dense jungle regrows constantly. Need hospital early.

### Desert / Extreme Desert
- Very low disease (0.7/year)
- Soft sand patches: 48% move speed, can't build on
- Extreme desert: **zero foraging**, must grow/hunt everything

### Tundra
- Temperature: -50C to 25C
- Growing season: 20/60 days to never
- **Must rely on hunting + hydroponics. Low disease.**

### Ice Sheet
- Temperature: -70C to 10C. Growing season: **never**
- **No outdoor farming. Hydroponics mandatory.**
- All food from hunting or indoor growing.

### Swamp Biomes (Temperate/Tropical/Cold Bog)
- Movement difficulty 4 (6 in winter)
- Marshy terrain requires **bridges** (12 wood each)
- High disease rates
- Cannot place stone walls on bridges — wood only over water/marsh

## Biome Planning for Agents

- **Food security**: check growing season length. Plan hydroponics if < 20 days.
- **Disease**: tropical biomes need hospital early.
- **Building materials**: desert/arid = less wood, prioritize stonecutting.
- **Defense**: swamp biomes slow enemies but also slow your colonists.
- **Temperature**: cold biomes need heating infrastructure, hot biomes need cooling.
